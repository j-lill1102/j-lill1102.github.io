Does The Amount of Hits By A Team In The NHL Weigh Into Their Wins?

2022-03-31

Incredible, eye-opening and exhilarating are often words that are used to describe the game of hockey.

As the chilly air of the arena brushes upon your face, your heart beats faster and faster as each moment toward puck-drop passes by. There are so many aspects of this wonderful game that draw sellout crowds all across the United States of America. Whether Alex Ovechkin is breaking scoring titles year after year or Marc Andre-Fleury’s graceful, yet stupendous saves, are taking a crowd’s breath away, the most flashy parts of the game may not be the only driving factors into determining a team’s success.

One of those driving factors: hits.

Hit and the aggressiveness that comes with it is just as big of a part of hockey as scoring and passing are. In hockey, you typically have four lines of forwards, with three players on each. Lines three and four typically have your scrappy, hard-hitting players.

The reasoning: those players could be a couple bad plays away from being scratched from the lineup on any given night.

Forwards tend to not be that tall and strong, so the method of “playing bigger than you are” tends to come into play in this instance. Every play, every second of ice time is made the most by each of the those players. Not to mention, with the defenseman on a roster, they tend to be bigger, stronger and taller, allowing them to act more as enforcers on the ice.

The main question that needs to be asked: Do the amount of hits a team in the NHL has factor into their success?

Now, take a look at each team’s hit totals up to this point in the 2021-22 season.
