Does The Amount of Hits By A Team In The NHL Weigh Into Their Wins?

2022-03-31

Incredible, eye-opening and exhilarating are often words that are used to describe the game of hockey.

As the chilly air of the arena brushes upon your face, your heart beats faster and faster as each moment toward puck-drop passes by. There are so many aspects of this wonderful game that draw sellout crowds all across the United States of America.

Whether Alex Ovechkin is breaking scoring titles year after year or Marc Andre-Fleury’s graceful, yet stupendous saves, are taking a crowd’s breath away, the most flashy parts of the game may not be the only driving factors into determining a team’s success.

One of those driving factors: hits.

Hits and the aggressiveness that comes with it is just as big of a part of hockey as scoring, passing and flashy saves are. In hockey, players might have a scrappy, hard-hitting attitude about their persona that give their team an edge in the hitting department. Some players are fighting for playing time, so every second on the ice is crucial.

It is teams in the NHL, comprised of those kinds of players, that always find a way to make a run at the Stanley Cup.

The main question that needs to be asked: Do the amount of hits a team in the NHL has factor into their success?

Now, take a look at each top 10 team’s hit totals up to this point in the 2021-22 season.

The Florida Panthers, currently the top team in the NHL, are currently sitting at sixth on the top 10 list for hits. The New York Rangers and the Boston Bruins, who round out the fourth and fifth ranked teams in the NHL standings, are also in the top 10 for hits in the NHL. There may be something to be said about the correlation between those three teams’ success and and their aggressiveness.

Below, the graph will attempt to show that very correlation between points and hits in the NHL. Points, for those who don’t know, are comprised of wins and overtime losses. You receive two points for a win and one point for an overtime loss. The more points you have, the higher you will be in the standings. Teams highlighted in red are in the playoffs as of today.

As you can see, 16 of the 17 teams above the hitting average are playoff teams. The one non-contending team in that spot: the Vegas Golden Knights. Take a look at the dot in the bottom right corner. That’s the Ottawa Senators, who have accumulated a league-high 2,079 hits, but a dismal 58 points.

Jumping back to October, hits may not be the biggest factor in the game in October and November. It’s the start of a new season and that aspect of the game may not be on players’ minds. Or is it on their minds? Now, come March and April, hits may be adding up left and right with the playoffs right around the corner.

As you can see, out of the 10 worst teams in the standings, as of April 7, 2022, only the Detroit Red Wings have accrued more wins in the last two months of the season than in the first two.

As for the wild card teams, Nashville heavily increased their hitting numbers over this time period, being the only wild card team to do so. Tampa Bay and Washington’s hitting number have dropped dramatically, which might be pointing to the reason while they are on the edge of the playoffs.

Lastly, of the 12 playoff teams, only seven improved their hitting. You may be wondering why! Well, a lot of teams, who at the top of their divisions, may ease play on the ice so they can save their stamina for the playoffs.

With the run to the greatest trophy in all of the sports, the Stanley Cup, will we see a plethora of hits from all 16 teams?

I would count on it.
