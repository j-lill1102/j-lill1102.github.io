What Made The Detroit "Bad Boy" Pistons So Bad?

2022-04-26

The Detroit “Bad Boy” Pistons. Made up of Isiah Thomas, Bill Laimbeer, Dennis Rodman, John Salley, Joe Dumars and more, the fierce, two-time, back-to-back NBA champions from the “Motor City” ruled basketball in the late 1980s and early 1990s. Though, their playing style was not taken to NBA fans and cities, besides Detroit. Their unorthodox style of play was not considered “Showtime” like the Lakers nor pure domination in every area like the Boston Celtics. So, then what did make these Pistons so “bad?” The combination of aggressive play on the court tied in with their superb defensive skills gave them the opportunity to take over the league with a new style of play.

During this time, Detroit was in the top two for most wins in each of the two seasons they won titles.

The Pistons won back-to-back titles in the 1988-89 and the 1989-90 seasons. Detroit’s blue-collared and tough basketball philosophy, preached to them by the late, great, Chuck Daly, gave them an edge in a lot of areas that teams in the NBA didn’t primarily focus on.

One of those areas: rebounding.

It’s such a big part of the game of basketball that you can’t really win without it. However, the saying does go, “Offense wins you games, defense wins you championships.” This was the reason they were so utterly dominant in the two seasons they won championships. With their defense came their play on the glass. Crashing the boards was what they did best. They were top 10 in defensive rebounds and total rebounds in both of those seasons. As a team that was in the bottom half of the league in points per game, their constant push to get boards gave them extra chances when, if rebounding wasn’t such a big part of their game, they shouldn’t have.
